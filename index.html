Member Update

Reconciliation 3.1:
The Vehicle for Once-in-a-Generation Health Reform

For several months, our coalition has laid the groundwork for health reform, waiting for the next major legislative opportunity.

That moment has now arrived. With DHS funded and the shutdown over, Hill discussions have shifted to "Reconciliation 3.0," a budget process poised to be the primary vehicle for substantive health reform this Congress. Crucially, this process is not subject to the filibuster, meaning it requires only 50 votes to pass in the Senate.

This represents a rare, once-in-a-generation chance to advance our collective goals. Our recent work ensures our reforms are ready for integration into this emerging package as it develops over the coming weeks.

Next Wednesday, we're convening a member call to walk through reconciliation timing, review our four-pillar framework, and plan our next moves together.

On this call, we will:

  • Walk through the updated four-pillar framework in the context of Reconciliation 3.0;
  • Share the latest expectations on timing and process from the Hill;
  • Discuss how each of you can help expand our coalition and execute our plan.
What we're asking from you:
  • Bring ideas tied to each of the four pillars — what should be prioritized, and where you see the biggest opportunities.
  • Bring people — current or prospective members you believe should be at the table as Reconciliation 3.0 takes shape.
    • Spokespeople for the media: Identify leaders prepared to go on the record to champion our pillars publicly, as well as experts available to speak on background to brief reporters and shape the unfolding narrative.
  • Bring partners — donors, organizations, or allies who can help us scale our impact and sustain this effort.

Our goal is to ensure that when reporters, staffers, or policymakers are looking for perspective on this vehicle, our coalition is seen as a go‑to source on what meaningful health reform could look like.

A calendar invite with time, platform, and dial-in details will follow.
